About Jomo Kenyatta

Jomo Kenyatta (c. 1897 – 22 August 1978) was a Kenyan anti-colonial activist and politician who governed Kenya as its first Prime Minister (1963–1964) and President (1964–1978). He played a leading role in Kenya's struggle for independence from British colonial rule.

As a key figure in the Kenyan African National Union (KANU), Kenyatta is often referred to as the "Father of the Nation." He promoted unity and economic development, laying the foundation for a new nation after decades of colonial exploitation.

Legacy

  • First President of Kenya
  • Promoted African nationalism
  • Fought for Kenya's independence
  • Established key institutions still in place today
